Taking an ancient approval chain process to the web

Users find these certifications mixed into an official document updated twice per year. When a manager and their employee agree the employee deserves a certification, they print and fill out a form.

The manager travels to their manager’s office

The 2nd Level Manager fills out their part of the form. They then send the form to an office where someone will add the certification to the employee’s personal record.

Clash between product and development teams!

We built an approval chain tool 3 years ago. Product folks say “build upon what we already built.” Developers disagee “you can’t decide that for us!”

Screenshot: Mapping processes

Converted process steps into work items for devs

My process steps don’t specify the “how” so devs are able to understand and talk out the problem without product folks imposing on them.

See the work items from the mapping

Interactive Prototype: User requesting a certification

We agree to build upon what we already built

Anyone can make suggestions, but the environment sets the flexibility for the whole team to be part of the conversation from idea to production. See what that led to me drawing up! Our agreed upon “how.”

View Results in Figma

Users have new certifications on their resumes with half the battle

In conclusion, this project tested my facilitation skills when developers refused to accept the initially presented how. Some development teams are flexible and prefer to simply build the how that’s asked for. Other development teams prefer you show them the work you took to get to the how so they can be part of the conversation. Both can be helpful at different times. I’m always prepared to flex to accommodate my teammates.
